Experiment

The impact of healthy aging on circulating immune cells and their environment is poorly understood. Here, we report transcriptomic, epigenomic (DNA methylation and chromatin profiling for five major histone modifications) proteomic, and metabolomic data for plasma and classical monocytes, isolated from healthy cohorts of young and older adults without inflammatory health conditions with comparable body-mass indices. In order to perform multi-omics analysis from single blood draws, we have optimized ultra-low-input ChIP-seq (ULI-ChIP-seq) data acquisition and analysis pipelines, and also developed novel computational approaches to deal with the natural variability of ULI-ChIP-seq profiles for multiple human samples.
Our data revealed that healthy aging was accompanied by significant differences in the metabolic and proteomic composition of plasma, as well as DNA methylation levels in monocytes, but no significant changes in transcriptomes or histone modifications. However, integrative analysis of the specific chromatin patterns revealed distinct positional clues to age-associated hyper- and hypo-methylation of DNA.



Analysis

To our knowledge, the comparative epigenetic study of this scale has never been undertaken previously in the context of human aging. Since the peak calling routine presents a significant challenge in working with large scale human epigenetic data, we developed a novel semi-supervised peak calling approach applicable to datasets of this scale.
Along with a pioneer dataset, we provide a completely new integrative solution for ChIP-seq analysis.
